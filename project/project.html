What is Global Warming

Global Warming is a gradual increase in the overall temperature of the earth's atmosphere due to the greenhouse effect. In addition, the greenhouse is catalogued or caused by the increased quatities of gases. Furthermore, the increases of gases is derived from the Carbon Dioxide (CO2) that cars exhale as they burn fuel. Besides, these gases trap the heat from the sun, and they are stuck on the earth's atmosphere. The result from the gases being on the atmosphere is an increase temperature on the earth.

Origins of Global Warming

Evidence of global warming started to show up when scientists started to measure Carbon Dioxide (CO2) readings in the late 1950s. They basically measure the readings by producing concentration curves graphically. In addition, the scientists recorded the data and comparing them. As years progressed, technology continue to evolve and offer scientists better and advanced tools to record data more efficiently. Lastly, they compared the data from decades, and they noticed an increased temperature ratio.
